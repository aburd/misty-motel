Spirituality

Eastern Windows

by Willow Tree Farmington

IN my travels, I have collected the aphorisms of many cultures in my Moleskine journal. I bring back to my materialistc Western children this ancient wisdom, applied practically to great effect by our international contemporaries in these personal translations.

Tibet: He who goes not down to the banks of the river. He who does, goes.

Thailand: How can the booby, if he cannot?

Czechoslovakia: Water in the cistern is worth as much as water in the bowl.

North Korea: Many die too late and some die too early.
Still the doctrine sounds strange: ‘Die at the right time.’
… Die at the right time: thus Zarathustra teaches.

Meditate on these and nothing more.
Peace Pit,
W. T. F.
